## NeurIPS 2025 Workshop Reports

> Issue [#29](https://github.com/inoue0426/Paper-reading/issues/29)

### AI for Life Sciences at NeurIPS Workshop 2025: Toward Biological World Models & AI Scientists

Across NeurIPS 2024–2025, research in AI for Life Sciences shows a unified and unmistakable trajectory:

**The field is shifting from predictive models toward intervention-aware, mechanistic, dynamic "Biological World Models"—and toward multi-agent systems functioning as AI Scientists.**

This transformation is driven by:

- Large-scale multimodal Foundation Models (FMs)
- Graph-based and causal representations
- Explosion of perturbation-labeled single-cell datasets
- Dynamic modeling (diffusion, ODEs, Schrödinger bridges)
- Multi-agent reasoning frameworks for hypothesis formation
- Spatial omics and virtual tissue modeling
- Domain-specialized BioFM and ChemFM surpassing LLMs

**The end goal is not accuracy, but mechanistic understanding, simulation, counterfactual reasoning, and scientific discovery.**

#### 1. Background: From Pattern Recognition → Mechanistic AI

Traditional ML in biology focused on correlations.
However, biology is **mechanistic, causal, and intervention-driven**.

NeurIPS has signaled a paradigm shift:

> **AI must model mechanisms, not correlations.**

#### 2. Stage 1 — Sequence Foundation Models (Protein/RNA/DNA)

These models encode:

- Protein language
- RNA/DNA sequence patterns
- Evolutionary and structural signals

They serve as the base **"vocabulary"** of biological modeling.

**Limitation:** Sequence alone cannot represent pathways, perturbation effects, or cell states.

#### 3. Stage 2 — Graph Foundation Models (Pathways, Networks, Causality)

Biology is inherently graph-structured.

| Biological System      | Graph Representation |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| Gene regulation        | GRN                  |
| Protein interactions   | PPI                  |
| Metabolism             | Reaction graph       |
| Cellular neighborhoods | Spatial graph        |
| Cross-species modeling | Multi-species graphs |

**Examples:**
- MoAgent (multi-agent causal MoA discovery)
- CentaurGNN (animal → human translation)
- Knowledge-graph-driven perturbation models

#### 4. Stage 3 — The Emergence of the Virtual Cell

A **Virtual Cell** is a mechanistic, multimodal, dynamic world model of cellular behavior.

**Capabilities:**
- Integrates multimodal datasets
- Models gene → pathway → cell transitions
- Learns drug, genetic, and environmental perturbation effects
- Represents dynamic trajectories
- Aligns with known biological mechanisms

**Examples:** EPFL Virtual Tissue FM, Arc Institute "STATE", ETH perturbation-aware models, IBM Coherent Virtual Cell, SandboxAQ Causal Virtual Cell, CZI Biohub single-cell diffusion models

#### 5. Stage 4 — Perturbation-Aware, Mechanistic & Dynamic FMs

Single-cell perturbation datasets have exploded:

| Dataset          | Cells   | Contexts | Perturbations |
|------------------|---------|----------|---------------|
| Replogle–Nadig   | ~1.5M   | 4        | ~8k           |
| X-Atlas–Orion    | ~8M     | 2        | ~19k          |
| Parse PBMC       | ~10M    | 18       | 90            |
| Tahoe-100M       | ~100M   | 50       | 1138          |

**Key Modeling Tools:** Diffusion models for expression dynamics, Neural ODEs for tumor/cell trajectories, Schrödinger Bridges for path inference

#### 6. Stage 5 — Biological World Models & Causal Evaluation

> **Accuracy is no longer the main metric; causal validity is.**

**New Evaluation Dimensions:** Intervention validity, Counterfactual consistency, Mechanistic alignment, Trajectory faithfulness, Biological pathway coherence

#### 7. Stage 6 — Multi-Agent Scientific Reasoning (AI Scientist)

**Examples:** MoAgent (multi-agent MoA discovery), SigSpace (LLM-based drug signature interpretation), Fauna Bio agent workflows, AIDO Lab (World Model + autonomous reasoning agents)

**Agent Capabilities:** Literature mining, Causal inference, Hypothesis generation, Virtual experimentation, Experimental planning, Iterative refinement

#### 8. Stage 7 — Rise of BioFM and ChemFM Over General LLMs

NeurIPS results show:
- GPT/Claude fail on ADMET tasks
- Specialized FMs (Nach0-1B, TxGamma) outperform dramatically

**Biology and chemistry require structure-, physics-, and mechanism-aware models.** General LLMs are insufficient for real scientific tasks.

#### 9. Spatial Omics FMs and Virtual Tissues

**Examples:** DeepSpot2Cell (H&E → virtual spatial transcriptomics), Spatial proteomic FMs (EPFL)

**Capabilities:** Virtual staining, Tumor microenvironment modeling, Tissue-level Virtual World Models

#### 10. Integrated Roadmap

```
Sequence FMs
↓
Graph FMs (causal structure)
↓
Virtual Cell (biological world model)
↓
Perturbation-aware & Mechanistic FMs
↓
Virtual Tissue / Virtual Patient
↓
Multi-Agent AI Scientist
↓
Semi-automated discovery, experiment planning & drug design
```

## ML in DD @ Broad Institute

> Issue [#28](https://github.com/inoue0426/Paper-reading/issues/28)

Reference: https://www.broadinstitute.org/machine-learning-drug-discovery-symposium/machine-learning-drug-discovery

### 1. Limited Performance of Single-Cell Foundation Models (scFMs)

Recent independent studies consistently show that current single-cell foundation models underperform, especially in **zero-shot** or **out-of-distribution (OOD)** scenarios.

**Key observations:**
- Zero-shot scFMs often perform worse than simpler baselines (HVG, Harmony/scVI, PCA)
- Fine-tuning hides many inherent weaknesses
- Performance limitations replicate across multiple labs and datasets
- Perturbation FMs do not consistently outperform linear baselines

**Implication:** Scaling alone is insufficient. scFMs do *not* automatically acquire biologically grounded structure; stronger biological or multimodal inductive biases are required.

### 2. Biological Data Are Hierarchically Structured Across Scales

Biological measurements form a **multi-scale hierarchy**: Bulk RNA-seq → Single-cell RNA-seq → Spatial omics

**Implication:** Single-modality models cannot reconstruct biology's interconnected structure. We need models capable of learning **multi-scale relationships**.

### 3. AI for Biology Must Be Multimodal

Single-omics foundation models fail to generalize adequately. Real biological prediction tasks require **multimodal, multi-scale integration**.

**Key points:**
- Disease mechanisms span molecular, cellular, tissue, organ, and clinical scales
- A recent MIT case study shows major performance gains when using multimodal data
- Effective biological AI requires massive paired multimodal datasets and architectures designed for biological structure

### 5. Biologically Driven Data Diversity Improves Model Performance

**Key findings:**
- Models trained on homogeneous datasets generalize poorly to new tissues or disease contexts
- Adding diverse datasets (bone marrow, cancers, perturbations) improves cross-context generalization
- Biological variability is *not noise* — it is an essential signal

## vec2vec – NeurIPS 2025

> Issue [#27](https://github.com/inoue0426/Paper-reading/issues/27)

**[Vec2Vec](https://openreview.net/forum?id=jiCLUPq5xv)** — Accepted for NeurIPS 2025

Unsupervised embedding-to-embedding translation without paired data or encoder access.

### Pros
- **Novel setting & method:** Unsupervised embedding-to-embedding translation without paired data or encoder access (vec2vec)
- **Broad, careful evaluation:** Multiple backbones (BERT/T5/RoBERTa/CLIP), OOD datasets (TweetTopic, MIMIC), metrics (cosine, Top-1, rank), plus attribute inference and inversion
- **Security impact:** Demonstrates concrete leakage risks from vector DB breaches
- **Ablations matter:** Latent-GAN, cycle consistency, and VSP losses are each necessary

### Cons
- **Training instability & compute heavy:** GAN training is seed-sensitive, especially cross-backbone
- **Overclaim/positioning:** Reads close to CycleGAN/word-alignment; "Strong PRH" under-theorized
- **Evaluation gaps:** Limited model coverage; zero-shot inversion not always strong
- **Baseline clarity:** OT/assignment baselines initially under-specified

## Causal & Geometric Representation Learning

> Issue [#24](https://github.com/inoue0426/Paper-reading/issues/24)

- **[Unpaired Multi-Domain Causal Representation Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.00993)**
  - NeurIPS 2023 — MIT, Broad, TUM
  - Proves that shared latent causal variables can be uniquely identified from unpaired multi-domain data. Provides theoretical identifiability conditions and a linear method for recovering the shared causal graph.

- **[The Latent Space Hypothesis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.04515)**
  - Arxiv — Oxford
  - Proposes that all medical data—genomic, imaging, physiological, and textual—are projections of a shared latent physiological space. Presents a conceptual framework that unifies multimodal medical foundation models via a latent space, linking biological hierarchy, interpretability, and causal reasoning.

- **[Score-based Causal Representation Learning: Linear and General Transformations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.00849)**
  - JMLR — CMU, Amazon, RPI, DeepMind
  - Examines how causal latent variables can be learned and identified when only observational data are available, but with implicit generative interventions. Proves identifiability conditions for nonlinear models and proposes a practical algorithm to recover the underlying causal structure.

- **[Identifying General Mechanism Shifts in Linear Causal Representations](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/hash/4ae163cb8788970e53b4fd9578141139-Abstract-Conference.html)**
  - NeurIPS 2024 — UT Austin, Chicago, CMU
  - Explores how to detect and characterize changes in causal mechanisms across multiple environments. Provides identifiability conditions for recognizing which causal relations have changed and proposes a method to recover the shared and shifted parts of a causal graph.

- **[Causal Representation Learning from Multimodal Biomedical Observations](https://openreview.net/forum?id=hjROBHstZ3)**
  - ICLR 2025 — CMU, Broad, U Bristol
  - Extends causal representation learning to multi-modal biomedical data such as gene expression, imaging, and physiological signals. Provides nonparametric identifiability theorems for shared and modality-specific causal variables.

### Peer-Reviewed Papers `Copilot`

#### NeurIPS 2023

- **[Judging LLM-as-a-Judge with MT-Bench and Chatbot Arena](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/hash/91f18a1287b398d378ef22505bf41832-Abstract-Datasets_and_Benchmarks.html)** `NeurIPS 2023`
  - The seminal paper proposing the LLM-as-a-Judge paradigm. Introduces MT-Bench (multi-turn question set) and Chatbot Arena (crowdsourced pairwise comparison), demonstrating that GPT-4 agrees with human preferences approximately 80% of the time. Systematically analyzes limitations such as position bias and verbosity bias.

- **[Benchmarking Foundation Models with Language-Model-as-an-Examiner](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/hash/f64e55d03e2fe61aa4114e49cb654acb-Abstract-Datasets_and_Benchmarks.html)** `NeurIPS 2023`
  - A framework that dynamically evaluates LLMs as an "examiner". Performs iterative question generation and scoring to assess open-ended capabilities that fixed benchmarks cannot measure. Allows multi-faceted verification of knowledge depth and response consistency.

#### EMNLP 2023

- **[G-Eval: NLG Evaluation using GPT-4 with Better Human Alignment](https://aclanthology.org/2023.emnlp-main.153/)** `EMNLP 2023`
  - An NLG evaluation framework using GPT-4. Clarifies evaluation criteria through Chain-of-Thought reasoning and achieves higher correlation with human judgments than ROUGE and BERTScore via form-based scoring. Effectiveness demonstrated on text summarization and dialog generation tasks.

#### ACL 2023

- **[Can Large Language Models Be an Alternative to Human Evaluations?](https://aclanthology.org/2023.acl-long.870/)** `ACL 2023`
  - A comprehensive study verifying whether LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT) can serve as substitutes for human evaluators. Compares annotation agreement rates, cost, and consistency with humans across multiple tasks, clarifying the effectiveness and limitations of LLMs for text generation quality evaluation.

#### ICLR 2024

- **[ChatEval: Towards Better LLM-based Evaluators through Multi-Agent Debate](https://openreview.net/forum?id=FQepisCUWu)** `ICLR 2024`
  - An evaluation framework in which multiple LLM agents reach consensus through debate. Reduces position bias compared to single-agent evaluation and improves agreement with human judgments. Demonstrates superiority on MT-Bench and FairEval.

- **[FLASK: Fine-grained Language Model Evaluation based on Alignment Skill Sets](https://openreview.net/forum?id=rKBBs4xPMc)** `ICLR 2024`
  - A protocol that decomposes LLM capabilities into fine-grained skills such as "logical reasoning", "factuality", and "instruction following" for evaluation. Identifies weaknesses at the skill level and visualizes inter-model differences hidden behind overall scores.

- **[PROMETHEUS: Inducing Fine-grained Evaluation Capability in Language Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=8euJaTveKw)** `ICLR 2024`
  - Open-source evaluator LLMs (7B and 13B) trained on feedback data with rubrics (scoring criteria). Achieves evaluation performance comparable to GPT-4 while being locally executable, avoiding reliance on proprietary APIs.

#### AAAI 2024

- **[LLMEVAL: A Preliminary Study on How to Evaluate Large Language Models](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/29934)** `AAAI 2024`
  - A multi-dimensional evaluation framework for Chinese and English LLMs. Systematizes the entire evaluation process including task design, evaluator selection, and scoring criteria, and conducts correlation analysis between GPT-4-based automated evaluation and human evaluation.

#### ACL 2024

- **[AlignBench: Benchmarking Chinese Alignment of Large Language Models](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.703/)** `ACL 2024`
  - A benchmark for evaluating the alignment of Chinese LLMs. Comprises 8 categories and over 700 questions, and introduces rule-calibration to GPT-4-based automatic scoring to reduce position bias. Widely used for comparing Chinese LLM capabilities.

#### ICLR 2025

- **[Justice or Prejudice? Quantifying Biases in LLM-as-a-Judge](https://openreview.net/forum?id=DJDPlpd36N)** `ICLR 2025`
  - A study quantitatively evaluating 12 types of biases inherent in LLM judges (such as verbosity, authority, and beauty). Measures the type and magnitude of biases across models and proposes debiasing methods for more reliable evaluation.

### Journal Papers `Copilot`

#### ACM Transactions on Intelligent Systems and Technology (ACM TIST) — 2024

- **[A Survey on Evaluation of Large Language Models](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3641289)** `ACM TIST 2024`
  - A comprehensive survey of LLM evaluation methods (ACM TIST vol.15, no.3). Systematizes automated, human, and benchmark evaluation including LLM-as-a-Judge, and organizes multi-dimensional evaluation axes such as knowledge, reasoning, code generation, and ethics. Covers over 200 papers and identifies future research challenges.

#### Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR) — 2023

- **[HELM: Holistic Evaluation of Language Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=iO4LZibEqW)** `TMLR 2023`
  - A comprehensive LLM evaluation framework covering 42 scenarios and 7 evaluation axes (Stanford CRFM). Proposes multi-faceted evaluation not dependent on a single metric and visualizes model tradeoffs. Establishes an evaluation design philosophy forming the foundation of LLM-as-a-Judge research.

#### EMNLP Findings (ACL Anthology) — 2023

- **[Large Language Models Are Not Yet Human-Level Evaluators for Abstractive Summarization](https://aclanthology.org/2023.findings-emnlp.278/)** `EMNLP Findings 2023`
  - A paper showing that LLMs (GPT-3.5/GPT-4) have not yet reached human-level performance as evaluators of abstractive summarization. Measures human correlation on dimensions such as consistency, relevance, and fluency, and clarifies the limitations and applicable conditions of LLM judges.

#### Information Processing & Management (Elsevier) — 2024

- **[Is ChatGPT a Good Sentiment Analyzer? A Preliminary Study](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0306457324000785)** `Information Processing & Management 2024`
  - A systematic evaluation of ChatGPT as a zero-shot sentiment analysis evaluator. Compares with existing SOTA across 18 datasets and 5 tasks, analyzing the effects of few-shot prompting, domain dependency, and bias. An empirical study demonstrating the scope and limitations of LLMs functioning as judges.

#### Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR) — 2024

- **[Chatbot Arena: An Open Platform for Evaluating LLMs by Human Preference](https://openreview.net/forum?id=a6CpElUsur)** `TMLR 2024`
  - An open LLM evaluation platform built by LMSYS (TMLR Featured Certification). Adopts an Elo rating system where users compare outputs of 2 models and vote for the better one. Rankings of major models including GPT-4, Claude, and Llama based on human preferences. Extended version of the MT-Bench/Chatbot Arena paper from NeurIPS 2023.

- **[PROMETHEUS 2: An Open Source Language Model Specialized in Evaluating Other Language Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=mAV4SFXIXU)** `TMLR 2024`
  - Open-source evaluator-only LLMs (7B and 8×7B) that succeed PROMETHEUS. Supports both direct evaluation and pairwise comparison, significantly improving correlation with GPT-4-based judges. Trained on rubric-annotated evaluation data to accommodate diverse evaluation criteria.

#### IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering (TKDE) — 2024

- **[Harnessing the Power of LLMs in Practice: A Survey on ChatGPT and Beyond](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10453110)** `IEEE TKDE 2024`
  - A survey systematizing LLM utilization in practical applications centered on ChatGPT (IEEE TKDE). Organizes evaluation methodologies across multiple tasks including text classification, information extraction, summarization, translation, and QA. Provides practical guidelines for using LLMs as judges.

#### ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR) — 2023

- **[Pre-train, Prompt, and Predict: A Systematic Survey of Prompting Methods in Natural Language Processing](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3560815)** `ACM CSUR 2023`
  - A survey systematically summarizing prompt-based NLP methods (ACM CSUR). Provides the theoretical foundation for evaluation prompt design widely used in LLM-as-a-Judge. Covers techniques used in evaluation tasks such as Chain-of-Thought and few-shot prompting.

#### Expert Systems with Applications (Elsevier) — 2024

- **[Can Large Language Models Serve as Evaluators for Code Summarization?](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0957417424003592)** `Expert Systems with Applications 2024`
  - A study automatically evaluating code summarization quality using GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 (Elsevier ESWA). Experimentally measures correlation, consistency, and reproducibility with human evaluation, clarifying the strengths and limitations of LLM judges in the code generation domain.

#### Artificial Intelligence Review (Springer) — 2024

- **[Large Language Models: A Survey](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-024-10808-0)** `Artificial Intelligence Review 2024`
  - A comprehensive survey of the current state of large language models (Springer AI Review). Covers architecture, training, fine-tuning, and evaluation methods broadly. Organizes the trends and challenges of automated evaluation including LLM-as-a-Judge from multiple perspectives, providing an overview for evaluation researchers.
